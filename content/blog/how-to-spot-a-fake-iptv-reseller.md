## How to spot a fake IPTV reseller

A fake or unreliable IPTV reseller usually shows clear warning signs: prices that seem too good to be true, untraceable payment methods, no free trial, no refund policy, no contactable support, and reviews that only appear on their own page. A legitimate service has secure payments, transparent terms, a money-back guarantee, real independent reviews, and support you can reach **before** you hand over any money.

The IPTV market is crowded, and alongside the genuine services there are sellers who take your payment and disappear — sometimes within weeks. Knowing what to look for protects your money and saves you the frustration of a service that dies mid-season.

## The warning signs of a dodgy IPTV seller

If you spot several of these, walk away:

- **The price is unrealistically low.** Running stable servers costs money. A "lifetime" plan for a few pounds is a classic sign the seller doesn't expect to be around long.
- **Only untraceable payment.** Gift cards, crypto-only with no alternative, or "friends and family" transfers leave you with no way to claim your money back.
- **No free trial.** A genuine service is happy to let you test the streams first. Refusing a trial suggests they'd rather you couldn't.
- **No refund policy.** Reputable services publish a clear money-back guarantee.
- **No support before you buy.** If you can't reach a human with a simple question now, you certainly won't be able to when a channel goes down.
- **Pressure and urgency.** "Only a few spots left" and countdown timers are designed to stop you thinking.
- **Reviews that all sound the same** — or only exist on the seller's own website, never on an independent platform.

## Protect yourself: a quick checklist

Before paying any IPTV service, tick these off:

- ✅ There's a genuine free trial
- ✅ There's a clear, published refund policy
- ✅ Payment goes through a secure, recognised checkout
- ✅ There are real, independent reviews (not just on their own site)
- ✅ You've reached support and had a fast, helpful reply
- ✅ The pricing is realistic, not too-good-to-be-true

If a service passes all six, you're dealing with a real business. If it fails two or more, keep your money.
