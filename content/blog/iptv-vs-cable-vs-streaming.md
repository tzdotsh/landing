## IPTV vs cable vs streaming: the short answer

**Cable/satellite** gives you reliable live TV but on expensive, fixed contracts with a dish or box. **Streaming apps** (like Netflix) are cheap and flexible but each only offers its own on-demand catalogue — and they don't do live TV or sport. **IPTV** combines live TV, live sport, and a large on-demand library in one place, over your internet connection, usually for a fraction of cable's cost. For most people who want everything in one place without a huge bill, IPTV is the most complete option.

Here's how the three compare on what actually matters.

::

## Cost

- **Cable/satellite:** the most expensive — £70–£110/month for a full package with sport, plus possible equipment and contract commitments.
- **Streaming apps:** cheap individually, but the costs stack once you have several, and you still have no live TV or sport.
- **IPTV:** typically the lowest cost for the most content — one subscription covering live channels, sport, and on-demand.

## Content

- **Cable:** strong live TV and sport, but limited on-demand and locked to set packages.
- **Streaming apps:** excellent original on-demand, but no live TV, no live sport, and you need several apps to cover everything.
- **IPTV:** live TV + live sport + a large on-demand library in a single guide. With Maxco that's 20,000+ live channels plus thousands of films and series — see our [IPTV VOD library](/iptv-vod) for what's on offer.

## Flexibility

- **Cable:** least flexible — contracts, a dish or box, an engineer visit, fixed to one home.
- **Streaming apps:** very flexible, on any device, cancel anytime.
- **IPTV:** flexible like streaming — any device, no dish, no engineer — with the breadth of cable.

## Quality

All three can deliver HD and 4K. With IPTV and streaming, quality depends on your internet (10 Mbps for HD, 25 Mbps+ for 4K) and, for IPTV, on the provider's servers — which is why reliability and anti-buffer technology matter when choosing one.

Cable has the breadth but the bill. Streaming apps have the price but not the live sport. IPTV is the option that tries to give you both.
::

## So which should you choose?

- **Choose cable** if you want a single traditional bill and don't mind the cost or contract.
- **Choose streaming apps** if you only watch on-demand box sets and don't care about live TV or sport.
- **Choose IPTV** if you want live TV, live sport, and a big on-demand library in one place — for far less than cable.

If that last one sounds like you, the best way to decide is to try it.

## Frequently asked questions

**What's the difference between IPTV and cable TV?**
Cable uses a physical cable or dish on a fixed contract; IPTV delivers TV over your internet to any device, with more flexibility and lower cost.

**Is IPTV better than streaming services like Netflix?**
They serve different needs — Netflix focuses on its own catalogue; IPTV combines live TV, sport and a large on-demand library in one place.

**Is IPTV cheaper than cable?**
Generally yes — cable with sport runs £70–£110/month; quality IPTV costs a fraction of that for more content.

**Do I need special equipment for IPTV?**
No dish or special box — it works on devices you already own.

**Which is best for live sport?**
IPTV brings the major competitions together in one place at a lower cost; reliability depends on choosing a provider with anti-buffer servers and high uptime.
