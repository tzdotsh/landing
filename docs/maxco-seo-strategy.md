## 1. The market in one picture (what people actually search & why they buy)

Across all five markets the same buyer psychology repeats, and it maps perfectly to your "Stop settling" positioning:

- **The trigger:** cost of traditional TV. UK buyers cite Sky/Virgin at £70–110/mo; Spain cites Movistar+; France cites €50+/mo bundles. IPTV at ~£5–15/mo is the switch driver.
- **The fear:** getting burned — buffering during the big match, services that vanish after payment, no support, scams. This fear is the #1 thing every comparison article addresses.
- **The decider:** reliability + support + a free trial to de-risk. Your real reviews are overwhelmingly about *fast, human support* — that is your single strongest, most defensible SEO/positioning asset.
- **The content:** live sport (Premier League, Champions League, LaLiga, F1, Ligue 1), device setup (Firestick, Smart TV), and "is it legal/safe".

**Strategic takeaway:** You will not out-spam the affiliate farms on "best IPTV". You *can* win on **trust-led, support-led, device-specific, and country-specific** content where your real reviews and real service are a moat.

## 4. AI-Search (AEO/GEO) optimisation — how to get *cited* by ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews

Traditional SEO gets you ranked; AI SEO gets you **cited**. ~45% of Google searches now show AI Overviews. Apply these on top of every page:

### 4.1 Structure (make content extractable)
- **Lead every section with a direct 40–60 word answer**, then elaborate. AI extracts passages, not pages.
- **Comparison tables** for any "X vs Y" (TiviMate vs Smarters, IPTV vs cable, plan tiers). Tables get cited disproportionately.
- **FAQ blocks** with natural-language questions ("Is IPTV legal in the UK?", "What internet speed do I need?").
- **Numbered steps** for all setup guides.
- **H2/H3 headings phrased as real queries.**

### 4.2 Authority (make content citable) — Princeton GEO research, ranked by impact
- **Cite sources (+40%)** — link Ofcom, Trustpilot, official broadcaster pages.
- **Add statistics (+37%)** — "20,000+ channels", "99.9% uptime", "4.8/5 from 334+ reviews", "support replies in minutes". Your real numbers.
- **Add quotations (+30%)** — real customer review quotes (you have them).
- **Authoritative tone, clarity, technical terms** — explain Xtream Codes, M3U, EPG correctly.
- **Freshness** — visible "Last updated: [date]" on every post; refresh quarterly. AI weights recency heavily.
- **Author attribution** — named author + short bio with relevant experience on each article.
- **Do NOT keyword-stuff** — it actively *reduces* AI visibility by ~10%.

### 4.3 Machine-readable files (add to site root)
- **`/llms.txt`** — short overview of what Maxco is, who it's for, links to key pages. (See llmstxt.org.)
- **`/pricing.md`** or `/pricing.txt` — plain-text plans/prices so AI agents comparing services can parse you. AI increasingly compares products programmatically before a human visits.
- **`robots.txt`** — ensure AI crawlers are ALLOWED: `GPTBot`, `ChatGPT-User`, `PerplexityBot`, `ClaudeBot`, `anthropic-ai`, `Google-Extended`, `Bingbot`. Blocking them = can't be cited. (You may block training-only `CCBot` if you wish.)

### 4.4 Schema markup (30–40% higher AI visibility)
- `Organization` (entity recognition for "Maxco"), `Article`/`BlogPosting` (every post), `FAQPage` (FAQ sections), `HowTo` (setup guides), `Product` + `AggregateRating` (plans + your 4.8/5), `BreadcrumbList`.
- **Fix the existing schema first** — your current schema-org had stale `localhost:3010` URLs, old "15,000 channels", and `hello@cccambox.com`. All must be corrected to maxco.one / 20,000+ / a Maxco email (see §6).

### 4.5 Third-party presence (AI cites where you appear, not just your site)
- Wikipedia mentions = 7.8% of ChatGPT citations; Reddit = 1.8%. You won't get a Wikipedia page easily, but: keep your **Trustpilot** profile strong (you already link it), participate authentically in relevant Reddit/forum threads, and pursue inclusion in legitimate "best IPTV" roundups where possible. YouTube setup videos are frequently cited by Google AI Overviews — a few real Firestick/Smart TV setup videos would punch above their weight.

## 5. ⚠️ Legal & compliance guardrails (read this before writing sport content)

This is not legal advice, but it directly affects SEO safety and brand longevity:

- **Don't claim official broadcast rights** to Premier League, Sky Sports, TNT, LaLiga, Champions League, Canal+, DAZN, etc. unless you actually hold them. Marketing copy that implies official rights is both an ASA/CMA (UK), ARCOM (FR), CNMC (ES) risk and a Google "your-money-or-your-life"/trust risk. Frame sport content around *how people watch / what to look for*, and let your channel list speak factually.
- **Rating honesty (already flagged):** display your **true** Trustpilot score (4.8 today), never inflated. Schema `AggregateRating` must match the real number. Regulators and Trustpilot both police this, and your site links straight to the live profile.
- **Channel claims:** keep them factual and verifiable ("20,000+ channels", "True FHD & 4K"). Avoid "all Sky channels", "every PPV free", etc.
- **Why this matters for SEO specifically:** Google's spam and YMYL systems demote sites that over-promise in regulated/risky categories. The *trust-led* content (legality guides, anti-scam guides, honest comparisons, real reviews) is simultaneously the safest AND the highest-converting — that alignment is your advantage.

## 6. On-page / technical SEO fixes (the audit — fix these regardless of content)

These are concrete issues already visible in your build, plus standard hygiene:

1. **`SITE_URL` → `https://maxco.one`** — your i18n `baseUrl`, canonical, OG, and schema all depend on it. Earlier renders showed `localhost:3010`. Confirm it's set in the server `.env` and rebuild; verify live page source shows `https://maxco.one` in canonical + schema, not localhost.
2. **Purge stale CCCAMBOX-era metadata** — schema-org / meta descriptions referenced old "15,000 channels" and `hello@cccambox.com`. Sweep all titles, meta descriptions, OG tags, and JSON-LD for any CCCAMBOX/old-figure remnants. Update channel count to your locked "20,000+".
3. **Title tags & meta descriptions per page** — write unique, keyword-aligned titles for home, channels, apps/install, FAQ, support, each blog post. Pattern: `Primary Keyword | Benefit | Maxco`. e.g. `Premium IPTV UK — 20,000+ Channels in 4K | Maxco`.
4. **Hreflang** — you have en-en + es-es with `prefix_except_default`. Ensure correct `hreflang` tags output for both locales (and add `x-default`) so Google serves the right language per market. This is critical for your multi-country play.
5. **Sitemap** — you have `@nuxtjs/sitemap` sourcing `/api/__sitemap__/urls`. Confirm it includes blog posts + both locales, and submit to Google Search Console + Bing Webmaster.
6. **robots.txt** — allow AI crawlers (see §4.3); ensure no accidental `Disallow` on important paths.
7. **Core Web Vitals** — you noticed ~1s content delay. Run Lighthouse; likely the large JS bundle/hydration + the `/v57` redirect hop (not the git layers — those are build-time). Improving LCP/TBT helps both SEO and AI Overview eligibility.
8. **Internal linking** — every blog post links to relevant product pages (channels, pricing, trial) and related posts. This is how you pass authority to commercial pages and how AI maps your topical coverage.
9. **Image alt text** — descriptive alt on poster/collage/setup images (also feeds AI Overviews and accessibility).
10. **`/v57` versioned routes & canonical** — make sure the canonical URL is the clean public URL, not the versioned/redirect path, so Google indexes the right one and link equity consolidates.

## 7. Branded-term consolidation (you already rank for these — protect them)

You rank for `maxco`, `maxco iptv`, `maxcotv`, `maxcotv app`, `maxcotv iptv`. Actions:
- **Pick one canonical brand spelling for SEO copy** ("Maxco") and use it consistently in titles/H1s/schema `Organization.name` + `alternateName` (list the variants as `alternateName` so Google links them to one entity).
- **Note the domain split:** brand searches may surface `maxcotv.com` (billing) and `maxco.one` (marketing). Decide which is the canonical front door for brand queries and make sure that one ranks #1 for "maxco" / "maxco iptv". Cross-link them and align their `Organization` schema so Google treats them as one entity, not two competing ones.
- **Trustpilot** (`maxcotv.com`) already carries brand authority — link it from schema `sameAs` so the entity graph connects your domains + reviews.
- **Defend the SERP:** a strong branded result + Trustpilot + your own pages should fill page 1 for "maxco", pushing down any reseller/scam impersonators (a real risk in this niche — ties back to your "Verify Subscription" feature).
